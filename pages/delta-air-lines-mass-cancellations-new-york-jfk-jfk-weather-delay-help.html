Common Excuses Airlines Give vs Actual Passenger Rights

Airlines often provide excuses for disruptions, but it's essential to know your rights and not accept vague explanations. Here are a few examples:

  • Airline excuse: "The flight was cancelled due to weather conditions, and we're not responsible for refunding your ticket." Actual passenger right: You may be entitled to reimbursement for the cost of a replacement flight or a refund of the original ticket price, depending on the circumstances.
  • Airline excuse: "The delay was caused by air traffic control, and we're not liable for compensation." Actual passenger right: You may still be eligible for compensation, as the airline is responsible for ensuring your flight arrives on time, regardless of the reason for the delay.
