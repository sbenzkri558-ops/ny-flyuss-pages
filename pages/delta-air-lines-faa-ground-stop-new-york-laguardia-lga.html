What Delta Air Lines Owes You Under DOT Rules 2026

The DOT rules are clear about the obligations of airlines towards their passengers. For flights within the US, if Delta Air Lines cancels your flight, you are entitled to a full refund, including any additional fees for services like checked bags or seat selection. The amount of this compensation can vary but is typically between $200 and $1,550, depending on the original ticket price and the airline's policies. For delays, the rules are less straightforward, but as a general guideline, if your flight is delayed by more than 3 hours, you may be eligible for compensation, which can range from $100 to $700, again depending on the specifics of your situation.

Common Excuses vs. Actual Passenger Rights

Airlines often use various excuses to deny passengers their rightful compensation. It's crucial to understand these tactics and know how to respond. For example, if Delta Air Lines claims that the disruption was due to circumstances beyond their control (like weather), but you believe this isn't entirely true, you have the right to question this assessment and seek further compensation.

Another common tactic is for the airline to offer vouchers instead of monetary compensation. While vouchers might seem like a convenient solution, they often come with restrictions and may not offer the same value as a cash refund or compensation. Be wary of such offers and consider seeking advice before accepting.
